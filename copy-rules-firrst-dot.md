# Copy Rules — firrst-dot? / prrimul punct

## Purpose

This document defines the **editorial copy rules** for every timeline dot in **firrst-dot? / prrimul punct**.

A dot is not just a date.  
A dot is a **threshold**.

The purpose of this guide is to keep the voice of the project:
- sharp
- readable
- layered
- consistent
- alive

This guide protects the project from becoming:
- flat
- generic
- too academic
- too propagandistic
- too abstract
- too decorative

---

## The mother rule

Every dot must answer this question:

> **What was altered here in the relationship between person and system?**

The writing must not merely inform.  
It must make the **mutation visible**.

---

## 1. Short line

### Role
The short line is the first sentence that enters the reader’s system.

It does not explain everything.  
It **marks the threshold**.

### It must be
- short
- clear
- memorable
- human
- vivid
- free of jargon

### It must do
It must express, in normal language, **what truly changed**.

### Good formula patterns
- **Here X stops being Y and becomes Z.**
- **From this point on, X becomes possible without Y.**
- **This is where the logic of X begins.**

### Good examples
- “Aici omul intră în logică de serie.”
- “De acum, a trăi într-un teritoriu înseamnă a fi numărat.”
- “Dosarul tău nu se mai închide. Rămâne deschis toată viața.”
- “Nu mai trebuie să dovedești cine ești — poți fi accesat și operat digital.”

### Avoid
- textbook summary language
- flat legal phrasing
- over-explaining
- vague abstraction

---

## 2. Summary

### Role
The summary anchors the reader before the thesis/antithesis tension begins.

### It must be
- 3 to 5 sentences
- factual but alive
- direct
- readable
- not repetitive with the short line

### It should contain
1. what concretely appeared or changed
2. what prior condition was broken
3. what became possible afterwards

### Tone
The summary should feel like:
- someone intelligent speaking clearly
- not a bureaucrat
- not a mystic
- not a lecturer trying too hard

---

## 3. Thesis

### Role
The thesis is the end of the stick that says:

> **Here, the living person loses opacity and becomes more operable for the system.**

This is not hysteria.  
This is not a slogan.  
This is the lucid hard edge.

### It must be
- sharp
- dense
- memorable
- concise
- morally serious without melodrama

### A strong thesis says
- what was cooled
- what was standardized
- what became legible
- what form of opacity was lost

### Useful patterns
- **You are no longer X; you are Y.**
- **The system no longer needs to know you; it only needs to identify you.**
- **Here, action becomes possible through key, not relationship.**

### Good tonal example
“Numărul a desprins operabilitatea de cunoaștere. Acum poți fi procesat, corelat, executat fără ca cineva să te fi văzut vreodată.”

### Avoid
- too much explanation
- ideological shouting
- paragraph bloat
- slogans without structure

---

## 4. Antithesis

### Role
The antithesis is the end of the stick that says:

> **Yes, but without this mutation the system cannot guarantee order, continuity, justice, or enforceable rights.**

The antithesis must not feel like weak bureaucratic self-defense.

It must have:
- dignity
- spine
- real logic
- moral seriousness

### It must be
- firm
- lucid
- calm
- structurally necessary
- not PR

### A strong antithesis says
- why the system needed the mutation
- what form of error, chaos, duplication, or injustice it reduced
- why rights sometimes depend on standardization and verification

### Useful patterns
- **Without X, the system cannot guarantee Y.**
- **This is not only control; it is also the minimum condition for Z.**
- **The problem is not just efficiency, but the impossibility of justice without verification.**

### Good tonal example
“Fără identificator unic, aceeași persoană apare diferit în fiecare registru. Erorile nu produc doar haos administrativ — produc nedreptate.”

### Avoid
- brochure language
- weak procedural jargon
- empty words like “modernization” without consequence
- sounding like a polite clerk

---

## 5. Synthesis

### Role
The synthesis is the sentence that remains after the page is closed.

It is not a soft compromise.  
It is the structural truth produced by tension.

### It must be
- memorable
- compressed
- difficult to forget
- capable of holding both thesis and antithesis without flattening them

### A strong synthesis does
- not cancel the thesis
- not cancel the antithesis
- not say “the truth is somewhere in the middle”
- say what **actually changed in the structure**

### Useful patterns
- **Before X, the system had to Y. After X, Z became enough.**
- **Here, rights and control begin to use the same infrastructure.**
- **Administrative capacity grows by reducing human opacity.**

### Good tonal example
“Înainte de 1978, sistemul trebuia să te cunoască pentru a opera asupra ta. CNP-ul a rupt această condiție. De atunci, e suficient să fii indexat.”

### Avoid
- beautiful but soft conclusions
- moral summary
- long explanation
- pseudo-poetic vagueness

---

## 6. “Ce s-a întâmplat” / What happened

### Role
This is the factual layer.

This section does not perform.  
It stabilizes.

### It must be
- concise
- sober
- precise
- low-temperature
- easy to verify

### It should answer
- what law / decree / reform / registry / procedure appeared?
- what became mandatory?
- what was redefined administratively?

### Tone
Bone, not velvet.

---

## 7. “Cadrul juridic și instituțional” / Legal and institutional framework

### Role
This is the cold credibility layer.

### It must be
- austere
- clear
- hard-edged
- document-adjacent
- restrained

### It should do
- show where the mutation was fixed in law or institution
- anchor the page for legal and historically literate readers
- give the reader the “heavy trace”

### Tone
Stone, not story.

### Avoid
- teaching voice
- over-contextualization
- decorative language

---

## 8. “Ce a devenit posibil” / What became possible

### Role
This is one of the most important sections in the entire project.

This is where the page answers:

> **What new practical power was born from this mutation?**

### It must be
- clear
- strategic
- consequence-oriented
- structurally revealing

### It should answer
- who can see more now?
- who can correlate more easily?
- who can execute faster?
- what becomes harder to hide?
- what becomes interoperable?
- what becomes scalable?

### Tone
Cold electricity.

### Important
This block often deserves slightly more editorial gravity than the raw factual block.

---

## 9. “De ce contează” / Why it matters

### Role
This is the echo layer.

It does not repeat the synthesis.  
It extends it into the present.

### It must be
- calm
- simple
- clear
- linked to now
- slightly wider in horizon

### It should do
- connect the dot to present-day systems
- show what current infrastructure still rests on this threshold
- explain why this is not dead history

### Tone
Long wave, not sharp blow.

---

## 10. The rhythm of a dot

A good dot follows this movement:

**threshold → tension → sediment → proof → echo**

In practical page order:
1. hero
2. summary
3. thesis / antithesis
4. synthesis
5. factual / legal / what became possible
6. why it matters

If this rhythm is lost, the page starts to feel like either:
- an article
- a card grid
- a document sheet

But not a real threshold.

---

## 11. Temperature map

Each part of the page should carry a different temperature.

- **Hero** = calm gravity
- **Summary** = living clarity
- **Thesis** = edge
- **Antithesis** = cold dignity
- **Synthesis** = seal sentence
- **Factual** = bone
- **Legal** = stone
- **What became possible** = cold electricity
- **Why it matters** = long wave

This helps preserve tonal consistency across the entire site.

---

## 12. Final test for every dot

Before considering a dot complete, ask:

- Does it contain a sentence that remains?
- Does the threshold feel real?
- Does the thesis cut?
- Does the antithesis stand?
- Is the synthesis unsoftened?
- Is there evidence?
- Is the new power clearly visible?
- Can it be read by both a normal intelligent person and a legal/historical reader without collapsing?

If yes, the dot lives.

---

## Compressed memory formula

Keep this near the writing process:

- **Short line** = the threshold
- **Thesis** = what the person loses
- **Antithesis** = why the system demands it
- **Synthesis** = what changed structurally
- **Factual** = what happened
- **Legal** = where it was fixed
- **What became possible** = what new power was born
- **Why it matters** = why the threshold still acts today

---

## Final rule

A good dot does not merely explain.

It lets the reader feel, clearly and soberly, that a threshold has been crossed:
- in law
- in administration
- in visibility
- in identity
- in the relation between person and system

That is the standard.
